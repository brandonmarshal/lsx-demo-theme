# Introduction

![Status: In progress](https://img.shields.io/badge/status-In%20progress-yellow)

This implementation plan covers a two-week sprint focused on completing the Figma Design System mobile navigation, prototyping nine main service pages, learning WordPress menu workflows, and building out AI Services and Content Services landing pages with their respective sub-pages. The work emphasizes component reusability, prototype connectivity, and animation/interaction design.

## 1. Requirements & Constraints

### Requirements

-   **REQ-001**: Complete mobile navigation menus in Figma Design System with full prototype connectivity
-   **REQ-002**: Collect or reuse components for 9 main service pages (Prototyping, Design, Development, Performance, Support, Training, Security, Hosting, Accessibility)
-   **REQ-003**: Attend WordPress workflow training session with Zared on Monday, April 20, 2026
-   **REQ-004**: Implement WordPress menu structure immediately after training session while knowledge is fresh
-   **REQ-005**: Build AI Services landing page and sub-pages with component collection/reuse
-   **REQ-006**: Build Content Service landing page and sub-pages with component collection/reuse
-   **REQ-007**: Include animations, hover effects, and clickable prototype connections for all pages
-   **REQ-008**: Conduct daily catch-up meetings with Zared to review previous day's work and address blockers

### Constraints

-   **CON-001**: WordPress menu implementation must occur immediately after training session (April 20) to maximize knowledge retention
-   **CON-002**: Timeline estimates are flexible due to uncertainty in task duration
-   **CON-003**: Work must accommodate daily catch-up meetings with Zared
-   **CON-004**: Menu implementation may extend into April 21 depending on complexity

### Guidelines

-   **GUD-001**: Prioritize component reusability across all service pages to maintain design consistency
-   **GUD-002**: Document any blockers during daily catch-ups for immediate resolution
-   **GUD-003**: Focus on completing mobile navigation before moving to service pages
-   **GUD-004**: Maintain prototype connectivity as pages are built rather than retroactively

### Patterns

-   **PAT-001**: Component-first approach: Identify reusable components before creating new ones
-   **PAT-002**: Prototype-as-you-build: Connect interactions and animations during page creation
-   **PAT-003**: Learn-then-apply: Implement WordPress menus immediately after training

-   **ALT-004**: Build AI and Content Service pages before the 9 main service pages - **Rejected**: Main service pages are higher priority and linked to homepage slider

## 7. Risks & Assumptions

### Risks

-   **RISK-001**: **Uncertainty in task duration** - Service page prototyping timeline is estimated; may need to adjust scope if work takes longer than anticipated
    -   _Mitigation_: Daily catch-ups with Zared to reassess timeline and reprioritize if needed
-   **RISK-002**: **WordPress menu implementation complexity** - Menu work may extend beyond April 20-21
    -   _Mitigation_: Block time immediately after training; document issues for follow-up in daily catch-up
-   **RISK-003**: **Component availability** - Existing component library may not have all needed components, requiring new component creation
    -   _Mitigation_: Conduct early audit (TASK-013) to identify gaps; reuse wherever possible
-   **RISK-004**: **Prototype connectivity complexity** - Connecting all interactions across multiple pages may be time-consuming
    -   _Mitigation_: Build connections as pages are created rather than retroactively
-   **RISK-005**: **Blocker escalation** - Daily catch-ups may reveal blockers that cannot be immediately resolved
    -   _Mitigation_: Document all blockers; have contingency tasks ready

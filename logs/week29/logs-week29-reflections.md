# Week 29 Log and Reflection

## Weekly Reflection

### Courses, Learning, and Key Activities

-   Worked extensively on the Lightspeed (LS) Design System, auditing and correcting text styles so they align with MA and LS typography.
-   Systematically collected components from key pages (Homepage, About, Contact) and applied Figma variables for typography, spacing, padding, and colors.
-   Updated and expanded LS documentation, including padding rules, margin policy, card styles (service cards, case studies, breadcrumbs), and a detailed checklist for collecting components and applying variables.
-   Learned and practiced improved workflows for organising components/patterns in the design file and separating out old Sixcats content.
-   Researched margin and spacing topics (negative margins, paragraph margins, and no-margin policies) to create a more robust spacing/margin approach.
-   Collected and documented reference designs from 21st.dev to inform LS patterns and components.

### What went well?

-   Fixing and standardising the LS text styles made component collection faster and more consistent across pages.
-   Completed collection and organisation of all Homepage components, and made strong progress on the About and Contact pages.
-   Meetings with Ash and Zared were very productive, giving clarity on margin and padding policies, when to create distinct title/subtitle components, and how to structure the design file for speed.
-   The new checklist and documentation tabs (especially for padding and margins) are already improving accuracy and reducing second-guessing while working.
-   Cleaning up the design file by removing Sixcats content and re-organising LS components/patterns has made the file easier to navigate and maintain.

### What have you learned?

-   A clearer understanding of when text needs its own component (e.g., unique hover states) versus when standard text styles are enough.
-   How to apply padding and section styles more correctly and consistently, especially for larger components like service cards that need more breathing room.
-   A more nuanced grasp of margin usage: when to avoid margins, where negative margins might appear, and how to capture these decisions in documentation.
-   How disciplined documentation (padding tab, notes tab, margin policy, card style rules) directly speeds up day-to-day work and reduces confusion later.

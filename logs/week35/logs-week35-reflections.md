### What went well?

-   **High output in key pages:** Built and integrated multiple services pages across three breakpoints, with dark/light mode checks and TOC updates.
-   **Better component discipline:** Rebuilding imported patterns (tabs, modal, related services) as component systems made the work more reusable and easier to align to the design system.
-   **Design system clean-up:** Correcting colour primitives, typography scale details, and spacing helped improve overall consistency.
-   **Briefing workflow started to scale:** Creating briefs + visual directions for many sections/pages gave clearer targets for improved designs and made it easier to produce example directions quickly.
-   **Practical knowledge transfer:** Seeing Zared’s Figma-to-WordPress extraction process clarified how patterns might translate into theme implementation work.

### What have you learned?

-   **Atomic Design theory → practical execution:** Translating "atoms/molecules/organisms" into real component sets and page patterns helps keep systems organised and scalable.
-   **Imported UI needs a systems pass:** Extracted designs from references (like 21st.dev) still require variable mapping, component breakdown, and state definitions to work in the design system.
-   **Design system QA matters:** Small mismatches (colours, font scale, padding) can compound quickly across pages—catching these early saves rework later.
-   **AI workflows need guardrails:** Briefing and image generation can be useful, but it requires strong constraints and iteration (and can still be inconsistent).

### Challenges Encountered

-   **AI consistency issues:** Repeated difficulties getting the design-brief agent and image generation to follow the required structure, and trouble producing sufficiently varied testimonial designs.
-   **Time lost to iteration overhead:** Re-prompting and correcting AI output created extra overhead compared to manual design iteration.
-   **Complexity of content parity work:** Comparing `Site.html` to content files required careful attention to detail and a defined workflow to avoid missing inconsistencies.
